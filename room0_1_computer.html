Level 0 — Section 1: What is a Computer?

Learn

A computer is a machine that takes input, processes it, and gives output. Hardware = physical parts you can touch. Software = instructions that tell hardware what to do.

See

Click each part to learn what it does:

Do

Look around your own computer. Identify each part physically. Say out loud: “This is the CPU. This is the monitor.”

Test

Without looking at notes, explain what each part does. If you forget one, click it again above.

Break

Imagine unplugging the monitor — what happens? You can’t see output. That’s a safe “break.”

Fix

Plug it back in. The display returns. That’s troubleshooting — finding what’s wrong and fixing it.

Explain

Answer out loud:

  • What is a computer?
  • Why does it exist?
  • Where will I use this knowledge at work?
  • How do I know it’s working?
  • What breaks if it’s wrong?

Apply to a Job

Help Desk: Identify hardware issues when users say “My computer won’t turn on.”

Field Tech: Replace faulty parts like RAM or hard drives.

Sysadmin: Understand how hardware affects performance.

Endpoint Engineer: Automate hardware inventory collection.

Interview Question:

“Explain the difference between hardware and software.”

Real Ticket:

User says: “My computer turns on but nothing shows on screen.” You check the monitor connection first.
